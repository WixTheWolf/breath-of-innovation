Sodium chlorite flavor science

Why TheraBreath is a hard flavor problem, which building blocks survive it, and how the July 8 mint platforms are designed around it. Background for the room, not a claims document.

The active: OXYD-8 stabilized chlorine dioxide

TheraBreath is built on OXYD-8, a stabilized sodium chlorite system that releases chlorine dioxide (ClO2) in the mouth. That releasing oxidizer is what neutralizes the volatile sulfur compounds behind bad breath. It is also, from a flavor standpoint, an aggressive environment. The same oxidative chemistry that destroys sulfur odors will slowly attack the flavor molecules dissolved alongside it.

The formula sits at a mildly alkaline pH so the chlorite stays stable on the shelf. Alkaline plus oxidizing is the combination flavor chemists least want to formulate into. It rules out most acids, most thiols, and many aldehydes, and it shortens the retain life of anything marginal.

What oxidation does to flavor

  • Aldehydes oxidize to acids. Bright top notes such as citral and cinnamaldehyde convert over time, so a fresh lemon or cinnamon lift fades and can turn sharp or soapy.
  • Thiols and sulfur notes are consumed first. Anything tropical, alliaceous, or roasted that leans on sulfur chemistry is the exact target of the active. It will not survive.
  • Terpenes cyclize and shift. Some citrus and herbal terpenes rearrange under oxidation, drifting toward turpentine or stale notes.
  • Color and clarity move. Oxidation can pull yellowing or haze, which matters in a clear rinse.

What holds up

The reliable lane is mint and the herbal, woody, and cooling materials that pair with it. These are the classes we lean on for anything meant to reach production.

Talking points for the room

  • Lead with the honest version: the active that makes TheraBreath work is the same thing that makes it a hard flavor job, and we formulate for it every day.
  • Frame mint as a strength, not a limitation. It is where the brand lives and where stability lives.
  • When a guest asks about a tropical or dessert direction, be candid that sulfur-forward and heavily aldehydic profiles fight the active, and offer the stabilizer path instead.
  • Point to retain data and QC clearance as the difference between a fun bench pour and a production-ready flavor.
